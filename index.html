LINER NOTES

There's a specific kind of satisfaction in shipping something that actually works — not just runs, but solves a real problem for a real person. That's what keeps me up at 2am, not the deadline.

I’m Rayven, a Software Engineer currently based at the University of Auckland. My technical DNA is a bit of a hybrid: I began my journey in Digital Media Technology at NJUPT, but driven by a hunger for deeper logic, I recalibrated my path to Data Science and Big Data. This transition granted me a unique duality—an intuitive eye for aesthetics paired with the cold, hard reasoning of data-driven systems.

After four years of rigorous data training, I moved into Software Engineering to focus on the "landing." I’m not just interested in how an algorithm runs in a lab; I want to see it ship. I find a specific satisfaction in bridging the gap between a complex mathematical formula and a tangible, industrial-grade product—whether that’s a pixel-perfect bilingual site or a high-speed CV tracking system.

Beyond the code, I consider myself a lifelong learner and a "pragmatic tinkerer." I thrive on the adrenaline of mastering new tools and maintain a high-efficiency output even when the pressure is on. In a team setting, I aim to be the steady hand—sincere, collaborative, and focused on moving the needle without the friction of ego.

When the screen goes dark, you’ll likely find me lost in the narrative of a good novel or working on something tactile and handmade. Whether it’s building a physical craft or piecing together a complex plot, I find that the patience and precision required for manual work often inform my approach to engineering. I believe the best software doesn't just speak to machines—it speaks to the human experience. The following "Sessions" are a record of my attempts to find that harmony.
